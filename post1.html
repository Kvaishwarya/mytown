Planning A Trip To Udupi? Make Sure To Include These Must-Do Activities

1. Visit the Udupi Sri Krishna Temple

The Udupi Sri Krishna Temple is the heart of Udupi and a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. This ancient temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, is renowned for its unique tradition where the deity is worshipped through a window with nine holes, known as the Kanakana Kindi. The temple complex is a bustling hub of activity, with rituals, prayers, and chariot festivals happening throughout the year. Don't miss the Paryaya festival, held every two years, which is a grand celebration marking the change of administration of the temple from one monastery to another.

2. Explore the Beaches

Malpe Beach, just a short drive from the town, is a popular spot for its golden sands, clear waters, and water sports activities. You can enjoy a boat ride to St. Mary's Island, known for its unique hexagonal basalt rock formations and serene environment. Kaup Beach, with its iconic lighthouse, offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea, especially during sunset. For a quieter experience, visit the Maravanthe Beach, where the Arabian Sea on one side and the Souparnika River on the other create a picturesque setting.

3. Experience Yakshagana Performance

Yakshagana is a traditional theater form of Karnataka that combines dance, music, and dialogue. Udupi is known for its rich Yakshagana tradition, and witnessing a live performance is a cultural treat. The vibrant costumes, elaborate makeup, and energetic performances bring to life the stories from Hindu epics and local folklore. Check for local schedules and catch a Yakshagana performance to immerse yourself in the cultural heritage of Udupi.

4. Bhuta Kola Ritual of Udupi

Bhuta Kola is a ritual of worshiping Gods performed by the Tulu community of Dakshina Kannada districts of Udupi. It is also performed in Tulu Nadu. The word Bhuta means spirit of supernatural power and Kola means a ceremony for powers. It is being followed from ancient times and is still followed in present day. In Indus Valley Civilization, Goddesses had been a sign of fertility and Bhuta Kola defines the worship of these goddesses.
